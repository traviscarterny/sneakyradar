GEL-KAYANO 14: THE STATEMENT SHOE

Kayano 14 Black Pure Silver

The Kayano 14 is a maximalist runner that looks like it was designed for a sci-fi movie set in 2004 — which is essentially what happened. Originally released as a performance running shoe, it's been adopted by the fashion world for its aggressive, technical aesthetic.

The Black/Pure Silver colorway was the #1 selling sneaker on StockX in 2025. It works because it's visually complex without being loud — the silver accents catch light in a way that makes the shoe look different depending on the angle.

Comfort is excellent thanks to ASICS' GEL technology in both the heel and forefoot. If you're on your feet all day, this shoe delivers. The tradeoff is weight — the Kayano 14 is noticeably heavier than the 1130.

Best colorways: Black/Pure Silver, White/Midnight, Cream/Sage. For resale value, watch for JJJJound and Kith collaborations.

GEL-1130: THE DAILY DRIVER

The Gel-1130 is the opposite philosophy: clean, slim, and versatile. Where the Kayano 14 demands attention, the 1130 complements whatever you're wearing. Its lower profile and simpler design make it the easier shoe to integrate into a wardrobe.

At $120 retail, it's also more accessible. Many colorways sit near retail on the secondary market, making it an easy recommendation for someone trying ASICS for the first time. The comfort is good but not quite Kayano-level — the 1130 has less cushioning technology, which contributes to its lighter weight.

Best colorways: White/Clay Canyon, White/Midnight, Black/Graphite Grey.

WHICH SHOULD YOU BUY?

Buy the Kayano 14 if: you want a head-turning shoe with maximum comfort. You wear wider pants or techwear-adjacent fits. You appreciate technical design details.
Buy the Gel-1130 if: you want a versatile daily shoe that works with everything. You prefer slim silhouettes. You're on a tighter budget.
Buy both if: you're all-in on the ASICS wave. The Kayano 14 for weekend fits and the 1130 for daily rotation is a strong combo.
