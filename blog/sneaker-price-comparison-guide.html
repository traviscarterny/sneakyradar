WHICH HAS CHEAPER PRICES?

It depends on the shoe. In general, StockX tends to be cheaper on high-demand, recent releases because of its bid/ask system that drives prices down through competition. GOAT occasionally wins on older releases, retros, and used pairs.

The difference can be significant. On a recent check of the Air Jordan 4 "Military Blue," StockX had the lowest ask at $215 while GOAT listed the same size at $238 — a $23 difference for the exact same deadstock shoe.

STOCKX: PROS & CONS

Pros

Transparent bid/ask pricing means you can see exactly what buyers are willing to pay and sellers are asking. Lower buyer fees than GOAT in most cases. Strong authentication with a dedicated verification center. Clean, data-driven interface shows price history and sales volume.

Cons

No used sneakers — deadstock only. No returns once your bid is matched. Occasional authentication delays during high-traffic drops. Shipping can take 10+ days since everything routes through their verification center.

GOAT: PROS & CONS

Pros

Sells both new and used sneakers, so you can find deals on pre-owned pairs. Larger catalog of rare and vintage shoes. Clean app with great product photos. "Instant Ship" option available for some pairs (pre-authenticated, ships same day).

Cons

Higher buyer fees than StockX (commission + cash out fees). Prices aren't always competitive on popular new releases. Used shoe conditions can vary. Less transparent pricing — no bid system, just ask prices.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is StockX legit?

Yes. StockX authenticates every item that passes through their platform. They've processed millions of transactions since launching in 2016. While no authentication process is perfect, StockX is one of the most trusted platforms in the resale market.

Is GOAT legit?

Yes. GOAT also authenticates every sneaker before it ships to the buyer. They've been operating since 2015 and are one of the two largest sneaker marketplaces in the world.

Can I return sneakers on StockX or GOAT?

Generally no. Both platforms operate as marketplaces where sales are final once authenticated and shipped. This is why it's important to compare prices before buying — you want to make sure you're getting the best deal the first time.

Which is better for selling sneakers?

StockX has lower seller fees (around 9-10%) compared to GOAT (9.5-15%). StockX also has higher volume for most popular releases, meaning your shoes sell faster. However, GOAT lets you sell used pairs, which StockX doesn't allow.
