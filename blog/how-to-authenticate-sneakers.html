HOW TO AUTHENTICATE SNEAKERS IN 2026

Updated February 2026 · By SneakyRadar
The Air Jordan 1 Chicago — one of the most counterfeited sneakers in history

Fake sneakers have never been harder to spot. In 2026, counterfeit factories are producing replicas so close to retail that even experienced collectors get fooled. Whether you're buying from a local seller, a Facebook group, or any platform without built-in authentication, knowing how to legit check is essential.

This guide covers the universal authentication checks that apply to every sneaker, plus model-specific tips for the most faked silhouettes.

THE UNIVERSAL AUTHENTICATION CHECKLIST

These checks apply to virtually every sneaker brand and model. Master these and you'll catch 90% of fakes.

01
Box Label
Check SKU matches the shoe. Font should be crisp and evenly spaced. Barcode must scan correctly. Size label inside shoe must match box.
02
Stitching
Even, consistent stitching throughout. No loose threads, uneven spacing, or crooked lines. Compare both shoes — they should be mirrors of each other.
03
Materials
Leather should feel premium, not plastic-y. Suede should have visible nap. Nubuck shouldn't feel like painted cardboard. Weight matters — fakes are often lighter.
04
Glue Work
Excess glue around the sole or midsole is a red flag. Retail pairs have clean lines. Some minor glue is normal on Nike QC, but excessive amounts indicate fakes.
05
Insole
Remove the insole and check the print underneath. Logos should be centered and sharp. Many fakes have blurry or off-center insole prints.
06
Smell
It sounds funny, but it works. Authentic sneakers have a distinct leather/rubber smell. Fakes often have a strong chemical or paint odor.

JORDAN AUTHENTICATION

Air Jordan 4 Military Blue — check the cage netting angle and tongue tag

Jordan 1

The most faked sneaker ever. Key tells: the Wings logo should have clean embossing with visible texture. The swoosh tip should point to the first lace hole. Toe box perforations should be evenly spaced in a consistent pattern. The hourglass shape on the back should be visible on deadstock pairs.

Jordan 4

Check the cage netting — it should be at a precise 45-degree angle with consistent diamond shapes. The tongue tag Jumpman should have all five fingers visible. The heel tab pull should be firm, not flimsy. Midsole paint should be clean without bleeding.

Jordan 11

The patent leather should have a mirror-like shine with no dull spots. The carbon fiber plate on the outsole should have a woven pattern — fakes often use painted plastic instead. The "45" or "23" on the heel should be deeply embossed.

YEEZY AUTHENTICATION

Yeezy 350 V2 and Yeezy Slide — two of the most replicated Yeezy models

Yeezy 350 V2

The boost sole is the easiest tell. Real Boost has a distinct texture with individually visible pellets that feel soft and bouncy. Fake Boost often looks melted together or too uniform. The pull tab (on models that have it) should be angled correctly — not straight up. The SPLY-350 text should be sharp and evenly spaced.

Yeezy Slides

Real slides have a specific flex pattern — they should bend but snap back. The EVA foam texture on authentic pairs has a slight grain. Fakes often have a smoother, more plastic-like surface. The size stamp inside should be clean and legible.

NIKE DUNK AUTHENTICATION

Nike Dunk Low Panda — the most produced (and most faked) Dunk of the decade

The Nike Dunk is heavily replicated, especially the Panda colorway. Key authentication points include the toe box shape (should be slightly chunky, not pointed), the swoosh positioning (tip should reach the third eyelet area), and the heel embroidery (NIKE text should be centered and evenly spaced). The tongue tag should have clean, consistent font with no bleeding or smudging.

NEW BALANCE AUTHENTICATION

New Balance 550 and 990v6 — both seeing increased counterfeiting in 2025-2026

New Balance counterfeits have surged alongside the brand's popularity. For the 550, check the "N" logo — it should have crisp edges with no paint bleeding. The perforations on the toe box should be clean circles, not ovals. On Made in USA models like the 990 series, the craftsmanship should be noticeably higher — check the suede quality and the "Made in USA" tag on the tongue.
