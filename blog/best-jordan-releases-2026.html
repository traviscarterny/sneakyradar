JORDAN BRAND IN 2026: WHAT'S CHANGED

Jordan Brand is taking a different approach this year. After years of criticism about oversaturation, they're reducing output and focusing on fewer, higher-quality releases. According to StockX data, average Jordan resell prices are already up 6% year-over-year — a sign that the scarcity strategy is working.

Nike CEO Elliott Hill has signaled that innovation is a priority, and we're expecting to see new cushioning tech integrated into retro silhouettes for the first time. The Jordan Luka line is also expanding, riding the momentum of Luka Doncic's continued dominance in the NBA.

For resellers, 2026 Jordans represent a return to reliable margins after a tough 2024-2025 period where oversupply killed profits on general releases.
